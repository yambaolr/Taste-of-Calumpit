History of Calumpit Longganisa

Calumpit Longganisa,

also known as Garlic longganisa, is a type of Filipino pork sausage that originates from the town of Calumpit in Bulacan, Philippines. This sausage is renowned for its distinct garlicky flavor, which sets it apart from other regional varieties of longganisa. The recipe typically includes lean pork, pork fat, garlic, bay leaves, brown sugar, soy sauce, vinegar, salt, black pepper, paprika, and sometimes chili.

The history of Calumpit longganisa is deeply intertwined with the culinary traditions of the region. Calumpit, being one of the oldest towns in Bulacan, has a rich cultural heritage that includes a variety of traditional foods. The longganisa from this area has been a staple in local cuisine for generations, often enjoyed during breakfast with garlic fried rice and a fried egg, a combination known as "longsilog".

The popularity of Calumpit longganisa has grown over the years, leading to the establishment of the Calumpit Longganisa Festival. This festival, first held in March 2023, celebrates the town’s founding anniversary and showcases the local delicacy through various activities, including a massive boodle fight featuring the longganisa. The festival has become an annual event, drawing thousands of visitors and highlighting the importance of this sausage in the local community

Calumpit longganisa’s unique flavor and cultural significance have made it a beloved dish not only in Bulacan but also in other parts of the Philippines. Its production continues to be a source of pride for the residents of Calumpit, who maintain traditional methods of preparation while also embracing new ways to promote their local specialty.
